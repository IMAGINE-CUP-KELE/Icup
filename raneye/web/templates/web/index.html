What we do

Detection and classification of wildlife

Our sensors distributed across the reserve sense motion and trigger the cameras to go on and take photos. The images taken are then classified using an AI model trained to classify them and sent to the website.

Heat signature reading

We use a passive infrared sensor to read the heat signatures across the reserve and compare them to the expected parameters. If they rise to an unexpected limit the concerned authorities are then alerted so as to avert disasters or greatly reduce the damage caused.

Notification of intruders to the concerned authorities.

In the case of an unauthorised intruder caught by the cameras, who could be a poacher or a logger, their photo is sent to the concerned authorities for quick action to be taken.

About

  • Our solution

    RANGER’S EYE Wildfires, deforestation and poaching are some of the activities that have led to the loss of animal life as well as plant life. The imprecision that is also involved when tourist visit game reserves as well as parks has caused many tourists to be disappointed leading to a depreciation in tourism, a major source of income for many countries . The ranger’s eye is a solution that seeks to ensure that all wildlife position, safety and forestry is well accounted for at any given time. The solution proposed utilizes a raspberry pi and a raspberrypi camera, and Passive Infrared Sensor as well as Sim808 for messaging or data transmission. It works by having these devices set up at frequented positions in the game park as well as the entry and exit points. All people with permission to enter the park must be registered into the system. When an animal or any motion is sensed, a camera comes on that can take a picture within its eye view and data from the picture is analyzed. Image detection and classification is done such that in case an animal is observed then a signal containing the name of the animal is sent to a database and later reflected on a web application indicating the animal position. In the case of a poacher, the data from the camera can identify one as an unknown or as an unexpected guest and therefore raise an alert through sending a signal to the IoT Central that is connected to an alert service. In case of a predicted fire, our Infrared heat sensor will determine the limits of the heat and then compare against the expected parameters. In case the parameters are exceeded then a signal indicating a distress is sent to IoT hub from where a pipeline that connects through the stream analytics and finally the powerBI dashboard allows this information to alert the concerned parties.
